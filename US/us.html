The United States' Role in World War II

The United States stood on the Allies side, along with France, the United Kingdom, the Soviet Union, and China. They united and fought against the Axis Powers, which were lead by Nazi Germany, Fascist Italy, and Imperial Japan.

Military Involvement

The U.S. fought on both the European and Pacific fronts. In Europe, American forces helped defeat Nazi Germany, notbaly through key operations like D-Day and the Battle of the Bulge. In the Pacific, the U.S. secured major victories at Midway, Iwo Jima, and Okinawa, and brought about Japan's surrender after dropping atomic bombs on Hiroshima and Nagasaki.

Support for Allies

Prior to its formal entry into the war, the U.S. provided critical support to Allied nations through the Lend-Lease Act, supplying weapons, equipment, and resources to countries like the United Kingdom, the Soviet Union, and China.

Post-War Impact

After the war, the U.S. emerged as a global superpower, playing a key role in establishing the united Nations and shaping the post-war order. The U.S. also contributed to Europe's recovery through the Marshall Plan and began the Cold War with the Soviet Union afterwards.
