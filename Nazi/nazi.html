Nazi Germany's Role in World War II

Nazi Germany, under Adolf Hitler, led the Axis Powers, which also included Fascist Italy and Imperial Japan. They united and fought against the Allies, which were led by the United States, the Soviet Union, the United Kingdom, France, and China.

Military Involvement

Nazi Germany fought primarily on the European and North African fronts. In Europe, German forces launched large-scale invasions, most notably through Operation Barbarossa, and engaged in brutal battles like Stalingrad and the Battle of the Bulge. In North Africa, Germany, under Field Marshal Erwin Rommel, fought to control key territories and oil resources but ultimately faced setbacks in battles like El Alamein.

Support for Axis Powers

Prior to its formal entry into the war, Nazi Germany forged alliances with Fascist Italy and Imperial Japan, providing military support and strategic coordination. Through the Tripartite Pact, Germany offered resources, weapons, and training to its Axis partners, while also coordinating military operations in Europe, North Africa, and the Pacific.
